# Fencing Technique and Tactics Knowledge

## Footwork Analysis

### En Garde Position
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Front foot pointing toward opponent
- Back foot at 90-degree angle
- Knees slightly bent
- Weight evenly distributed
- Guard at chest height
- Tip at opponent's valid target

### Advance
- Leading with front foot
- Back foot follows maintaining stance
- Smooth, controlled movement
- Stay on balls of feet
- Maintain ready position

### Retreat
- Push off from front foot
- Back foot leads the movement
- Stay balanced and ready
- Keep distance from opponent

### Lunge
- explosive forward motion
- Front knee over ankle (not past)
- Back leg straight and strong
- Front arm extended toward target
- Recovery should be smooth

### Recovery
- Return to en garde from lunge
- Front foot pulls back first
- Maintain balance throughout
- Be ready for next action

## Blade Work Analysis

### Basic Attacks

#### Straight Attack
- Simple extension toward target
- Weapon arm stays controlled
- Final extension is explosive
- Target the valid area

#### Cutover (Foil/Sabre)
- Blade sweeps over opponent's blade
- Change from high to low line (or vice versa)
- Attack on the change of line

#### One-Two
- Beat on opponent's blade
- Immediate attack after beat
- Creates opening for clean hit

#### Remise
- Second attack after first misses
- Continues forward motion
- Used when opponent fails to counter

### Defensive Actions

#### Parries
- **Simple Parry**: Direct block to deflect blade
- **Circular Parry**: Sweeping motion to control blade
- **Counter-Parry**: Anticipating attack direction
- Position: Strong wrist, controlled guard

#### Riposte
- Attack immediately after successful parry
- Can be direct or compound
- Maintain offensive initiative

#### Counter-Attack
- Attack into opponent's attack
- High risk, high reward
- Common in Épée
- Timing is critical

## Attack and Defense Transitions

### Offensive to Defensive
- Recognizing opponent's preparation
- Breaking distance
- Transitioning to parry position
- Creating time through footwork

### Defensive to Offensive
- Successful parry creates opportunity
- Quick riposte timing
- Counter-attack timing
- Using opponent's momentum

### Compound Actions
- Multiple feints before final attack
- Change of direction
- Change of tempo
- Disengagement

## Tactical Strategies

### Distance Control
- **Out of Distance**: Safe, observation mode
- **In Fighting Distance**: Dangerous, tactical range
- **Optimal Distance**: Lunge can reach, opponent cannot
- Use advances and retreats to control distance

### Timing
- Attack when opponent is committed
- Feint to test reaction
- Use opponent's recovery time
- Vary attack tempo

### Feint System
1. Initial feint looks like real attack
2. Opponent reacts (parry or counter)
3. Disengage to opposite line
4. Complete actual attack

### Attack Patterns
- **Simple Attack**: One clean action
- **Compound Attack**: Two or more actions
- **Attack on Preparation**: Before opponent's attack
- **Counter-Attack**: Into opponent's attack
- **Simultaneous Attack**: Both fencers attack

## Opponent Analysis

### Before Bout
- Study opponent's preferred techniques
- Note stance and habits
- Identify stronger side (hand, line)
- Watch for patterns in previous bouts

### During Bout
- Adapt to opponent's tactics
- Exploit weaknesses
- Note fatigue patterns
- Watch for technical breakdown

### Key Observations
- Which hand do they favor?
- Do they prefer one line (high/low)?
- What is their defensive style?
- How do they handle pressure?
- What are their go-to counter-attacks?

---

## Tactical Situations

### Against Aggressive Opponent
- Counter-attack opportunities
- Use their momentum against them
- Defensive parry-riposte
- Control distance carefully

### Against Defensive Opponent
- Create openings through feints
- Vary attack timing
- Use compound actions
- Make them react first

### Against Technical Opponent
- Simple, clean techniques
- Don't get drawn into complex exchanges
- Use unexpected tactics
- Maintain composure

### In Final Seconds
- Know the score
- Understand priority rules
- Take calculated risks
- Stay focused until final whistle

---

## Weapon-Specific Tactics

### Foil
- Work the lines (high/low)
- Use priority to your advantage
- Compound attacks to gain priority
- Parry-riposte is key

### Épée
- Counter-attack more freely
- Distance is your ally
- Be patient and tactical
- First touch wins

### Sabre
- Use speed and aggression
- Cut variations (face, chest, arm)
- Counter-attack into attacks
- Attack the arm

## Mental Tactics

### Between Bouts
- Reset mentally
- Review what worked
- Plan adjustments
- Stay confident but not arrogant

### During Bouts
- Stay present focused
- Control breathing
- Trust your training
- Adapt to situations

### Handling Pressure
- Process over outcome
- One point at a time
- Visualization techniques
- Positive self-talk
