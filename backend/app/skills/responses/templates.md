## Lunge (弓步)

The lunge is one of the most important attacking techniques in fencing:

1. **Start from en garde** - Feet shoulder-width apart
2. **Push off** - Drive from back leg
3. **Extend front leg** - Full extension at end
4. **Extend front arm** - Weapon pointed at target
5. **Land correctly** - Front foot heel first
6. **Recover** - Quickly return to en garde

**Key points:**
- Keep core tight and body low
- Maintain balance
- Practice recovery as much as the lunge itself

## Footwork (步法)

Good footwork is the foundation of fencing:

**Basic Principles:**
1. Small, quick steps
2. Weight on balls of feet
3. Quick recovery

**Essential Drills:**
- Advance-retreat patterns (3 min daily)
- Lunge-distance walking
- In-out movements
- Direction change drills

**Common Mistakes:**
- Heel-first steps
- Crossing feet
- Big, loud steps
- Staying flat-footed

## Parry (防守)

Parries are defensive blade actions:

**Key Principles:**
1. Use weak part of blade (near handle)
2. Keep wrist firm but flexible
3. Disengage immediately after parry

**Common Parries:**
- **4th Parry** - Inside line
- **6th Parry** - Outside line
- **Counter-parries** - Circular parries

**Practice Tip:** Always follow a parry with a riposte!

## Distance Control (距离控制)

Three zones in fencing:

1. **Out of Range** - Safe but can't score
2. **In Distance** - Can attack but risky
3. **Lunge Distance** - Optimal for scoring

**How to Judge:**
- Read opponent's body language
- Notice attack motion start
- Feel "comfortable" reaching distance

## Training Routine

**Weekly Structure:**
- Mon: Footwork focus
- Tue: Blade work
- Wed: Rest or light conditioning
- Thu: Tactics and sparring
- Fri: Technique review
- Sat: Competition practice
- Sun: Rest

**Daily Practice (1-2 hours):**
1. Warm-up (10 min)
2. Footwork drills (20 min)
3. Blade work (20 min)
4. Sparring (30 min)
5. Cool down (10 min)
